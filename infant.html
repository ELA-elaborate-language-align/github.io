We are here to help your infant develop:

  • make early attempts at speaking
  • learn to understand a variety of words by listening, observing, and engaging
  • use sounds and gestures to express needs, wants, and interests
  • listen to books read aloud
.

Executive Function

  • focus attention for short periods of time.
  • use different approaches to solve simple gestures, such as holding age appropriate toys for dexterity
  • control impulses

Social and Emotional development

  • from an attachment to familliar adults
  • begin interact with other children
  • express some emotions through sounds, facial expressions, and movements
  • build sense of self recognizing self in mirror

Physical Develpment and Wellness

  • work on a physical milestone like rolling over, crawling
  • coordinate hand movements to begin reaching for age appropriate toys or books and grasping them
  • start cooperate with care routines like getting dressed and washing hands
  • encouraging an interest in trying new foods age appropriate

Cognitive Development

  • begin distinguish between familiar and unfamiliar
  • encourage using all five senses to explore and observe
  • encourage copying simple sctions and gesture such as repeating words or trying to stand up and walk
  • perform actions to gain response

Creative Expressions

  • begin explore colors
  • explore variety of texture such as wet, warm, cold
  • encouraging expressing feelings through gesture
  • listen to music while experimenting age appropriate instruments

Developing a secure bond with their key person allows them to explore and play freely but feel safe within the environment. They will have activities and quality learning experiences planned for them based on their individual needs.

For the parents

The key person approach provides them with a dedicated staff member who fully understands the learning, development and care needs of their child, this gives you the confidence to leave your child at the nursery knowing they will be cared for in a way that is tailored to the information that you provide.

Sharing of information allows the key person to maintain a continuity of care and ensure collaborate partnership working between home and the nursery setting.

Settling in

At CleverLand your key person will meet with you during your 'settling in visits', during this time they will discuss your child's individual routines, the people who are special to them, their likes and dislikes and any other key information to ensure this is incorporated into the daily care of your child. Each key person has a sound knowledge and range of skills to enable them in providing parents with advice and guidance and supporting any concerns with starting nursery or completing transitions from room to room and on to school.

Your key person

Your key person will generally be the person who welcomes you and your child into the nursery and supports them in settling into the session. They will also be the main practitioner carrying out the child's intimate care needs, such as putting to sleep, diapers, spare changing clothes and physical closeness. Any information shared with the parents is also done mainly through the key person.

To ensure consistent continuity of care for all children, each key person is paired with a 'buddy' who will take on the role of the key person during periods of absence such as holidays. It is part of the key person's role to ensure that their 'buddy' and other staff within the environment are aware of their key children's needs and have a strong relationship with the parents to enable clear communication.
